Programming is a way of getting a machine to do your bidding.
Humans are pretty good at understanding what you want them to do, even when you give them ambiguous commands.
Computers are really terrible at figuring out what you mean. To get a computer to do your bidding, you've got to be very explicit about every step required to achieve your ends. Typing exactly the right characters matters a lot!
Consider the following button:
If you click it once, you get 5 points. However, if you click the button again, you don't get any points.
In programming and computer science, we might say that pressing the button transitioned the program behind the button from one state to the next. In the first state, clicking the button adds 5 points and moves to the next state. In the next state, clicking the button does nothing.
Computer programs can be thought of in terms of state machines ‐ a series of transitions and transformations from one state to the next.
